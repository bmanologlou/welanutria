Your weight on the scale tells you how heavy you are, but not what you're made of. Two people at the same weight can look completely different depending on how much is muscle and how much is fat. That's where body fat percentage comes in, and you can estimate yours at home with nothing more than a tape measure.

## Why body fat percentage beats weight

The scale lumps everything together: muscle, fat, bone, water. **Body fat percentage** separates out the part most people actually care about, telling you how much of your weight is fat.

This matters because two goals that look identical on the scale can be opposite in reality. Someone "losing weight" might be losing muscle; someone whose weight is steady might be replacing fat with muscle, a great result the scale completely hides. Tracking body fat shows what's really happening.

## The Navy method, explained

The most practical home approach is the **U.S. Navy circumference method**. It estimates body fat from a few tape measurements rather than expensive equipment, which is why our [body fat calculator](/calculators/body-fat/) uses it.

Here's what you measure:

- **Neck:** just below the larynx (Adam's apple).
- **Waist:** at the navel for men, at the narrowest point for women.
- **Hips:** at the widest point (women only).
- **Height:** your standing height.

Plug those in and you get an estimate in seconds.

## How to measure accurately

The method is only as good as your measurements, so a little care goes a long way:

- Use a **flexible tape**, the kind used for sewing.
- Keep the tape **level and snug**, without digging into the skin.
- **Don't suck in** or flex; stand relaxed and normal.
- Measure at the **same time of day**, ideally in the morning, for consistent tracking.

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>Consistency beats precision.</strong> Even if your absolute number is a little off, measuring the same way each time means the trend is reliable. For tracking progress, the direction matters more than the exact figure.</p></div>

## Healthy body fat ranges

What counts as healthy depends on sex and age. As a rough guide:

| Category | Men | Women |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Essential fat | 2 to 5% | 10 to 13% |
| Athletes | 6 to 13% | 14 to 20% |
| Fitness | 14 to 17% | 21 to 24% |
| Average | 18 to 24% | 25 to 31% |
| Above average | 25%+ | 32%+ |

These are general ranges, not strict rules. For guidance specific to you, speak to a professional.

## Body fat and BMI together

Body fat percentage is the perfect partner to [BMI](/calculators/bmi/). BMI gives a fast weight-category check but can't tell muscle from fat; body fat fills exactly that gap. Used together, they tell you both how heavy you are and what that weight is made of.
