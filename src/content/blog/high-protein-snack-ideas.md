Snacks used to be an afterthought. Now they are one of the easiest ways to hit your protein target, and the trend for high-protein snacking shows no sign of slowing. Here are simple, filling ideas, sweet and savoury, including a few of the viral favourites, most delivering 15 to 20g of protein.

Aim for 10 to 20g of protein per snack.</div>

## Why protein snacks work

Protein is the most **filling** macronutrient, so a protein-rich snack does more than pass the time: it curbs hunger, steadies energy and helps you hit your daily target. Pair it with fibre and you get lasting fullness on modest calories, which is why the [high-protein high-fibre](/blog/high-protein-high-fiber-foods/) combination is so effective for snacking too.

Not sure how much protein you need? Our [protein calculator](/calculators/protein/) gives you a daily target, and snacks are often the easiest place to close the gap.

## Sweet high-protein snacks

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>The viral one:</strong> blended cottage cheese "ice cream". Blitz cottage cheese with frozen fruit and a little honey until smooth, then freeze. It sets like soft-serve and delivers serious protein, which is exactly why it took over social media.</p></div>

- **Greek yogurt or skyr with berries and seeds** (a classic, ~15 to 18g protein)
- **Cottage cheese with fruit**, like our [cottage cheese and fruit bowl](/recipes/cottage-cheese-fruit-bowl/) (~28g)
- **Protein energy balls**, like our [peanut butter energy balls](/recipes/peanut-butter-energy-balls/)
- **A protein smoothie**, like our [protein berry smoothie](/recipes/protein-berry-smoothie/) (~42g)
- **Chia pudding**, like our [chia pudding with berries](/recipes/chia-pudding-berries/)
- **Frozen yogurt clusters:** spoon Greek yogurt with berries onto a tray and freeze

## Savoury high-protein snacks

- **Boiled eggs** (batch-cook a few, around 6g each)
- **Edamame**, lightly salted (around 11g per 100g)
- **Roasted chickpeas**, crunchy and high in fibre
- **Hummus with vegetable sticks** for protein plus fibre
- **A small bowl of leftover soup**, like our [chicken and vegetable soup](/recipes/chicken-vegetable-soup/) (~26g)
- **Cheese with an apple or oatcakes**

## Before-bed snacks

For an evening snack, **cottage cheese** and **Greek yogurt** are ideal: both contain casein, a slow-digesting protein that supplies amino acids through the night to support muscle recovery. A small bowl with a few berries is satisfying without being heavy. More on this in our [cottage cheese nutrition guide](/blog/cottage-cheese-nutrition/).

## Build your own

The formula is simple: **a protein base plus something with fibre**. Pick one from each and you have a balanced snack in seconds.

- **Protein base:** Greek yogurt, skyr, cottage cheese, eggs, edamame, a protein shake
- **Fibre and flavour:** berries, apple, seeds, oatcakes, vegetable sticks, a spoon of nut butter

## The bottom line

High-protein snacking is one of the simplest upgrades you can make: it curbs hunger, supports muscle and makes hitting your daily protein far easier. Keep a few staples on hand, Greek yogurt, skyr, cottage cheese, eggs, edamame, and pair them with fruit or fibre.
