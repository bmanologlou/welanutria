Cottage cheese has become the internet's favourite protein hack, and for good reason: it is cheap, packed with protein, and works in both sweet and savoury dishes. Here are the best ways to use it, from two-minute breakfasts to blended sauces, plus recipes to get you started.

<div class="key-takeaway"><strong>The short answer</strong>Cottage cheese works sweet (fruit bowls, pancakes, oats, smoothies) and savoury (blended pasta sauce, dips, on toast, in eggs). Blending it smooth is the trick that wins over anyone put off by the texture. Most cottage cheese meals land at 20 to 30g of protein per serving.</div>

## Why cottage cheese works so well

Three things explain the hype: it delivers around **12g of protein per 100g**, it is one of the **cheapest** protein sources in the shop, and it is genuinely **versatile**. Blend it and it turns creamy, like ricotta; leave it as is and it adds texture to bowls. Full nutrition breakdown in our [cottage cheese nutrition guide](/blog/cottage-cheese-nutrition/).

## Sweet cottage cheese ideas

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>The blending trick:</strong> if the curd texture puts you off, blitz cottage cheese in a blender until completely smooth. It becomes creamy and neutral, and works anywhere you'd use Greek yogurt or ricotta.</p></div>

- **Fruit and seed bowl** (the classic): our [cottage cheese and fruit bowl](/recipes/cottage-cheese-fruit-bowl/) delivers 28g protein in minutes
- **Blended into pancakes:** swap some flour for blended cottage cheese, as in our [banana protein pancakes](/recipes/banana-protein-pancakes/) approach
- **Stirred into overnight oats:** add a spoonful to [high-protein overnight oats](/recipes/overnight-oats-protein/) for extra protein and creaminess
- **In smoothies:** blend with berries and milk, similar to our [protein berry smoothie](/recipes/protein-berry-smoothie/)
- **Frozen:** blend with fruit and freeze for high-protein ice cream

## Savoury cottage cheese ideas

This is where cottage cheese surprises people:

- **Creamy pasta sauce:** blend smooth with garlic, lemon and pepper, then toss through hot pasta
- **Whipped dip:** blend with herbs, olive oil and lemon for a high-protein dip
- **On toast:** spread on wholegrain toast, top with a poached egg (like our [avocado and egg toast](/recipes/avocado-egg-toast/), swapping in cottage cheese)
- **Folded into eggs:** stir into [scrambled eggs](/recipes/egg-white-veggie-scramble/) at the end for creaminess and extra protein
- **In place of ricotta:** use in lasagne, bakes and stuffed pasta

## Cottage cheese or Greek yogurt?

Both are excellent, and many people use them interchangeably. Cottage cheese is slightly higher in protein and better in savoury dishes; Greek yogurt is creamier and higher in calcium. See the full [cottage cheese vs Greek yogurt comparison](/compare/cottage-cheese-vs-greek-yogurt/) to decide, or browse all our [high-protein recipes](/recipes/).

## The bottom line

Cottage cheese earns its viral status: cheap, high in protein, and versatile enough for breakfast bowls, blended sauces, dips and desserts. Blend it if the texture is not for you, keep plain tubs in the fridge, and it becomes one of the easiest ways to add 15 to 20g of protein to any meal.
