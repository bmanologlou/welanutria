## Why a food scale beats guessing

Portion sizes are deceptively hard to judge by eye. Studies consistently show people **underestimate how much they eat**, sometimes by hundreds of calories a day. A scale removes that guesswork. Whether you are trying to lose weight or build muscle, accurate input is what makes tracking actually work.

If you have ever felt your progress stalled for no reason, weighing your food for a week is often the fastest way to find the answer. Pair it with our [calorie deficit calculator](/calculators/calorie-deficit/) or [TDEE calculator](/calculators/tdee/) to know your target, then use the scale to hit it accurately.

## The features that actually matter

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>What to look for:</strong></p><ul><li><strong>1g accuracy:</strong> precise enough for all food tracking</li><li><strong>Tare function:</strong> zero out the bowl or container</li><li><strong>5kg+ capacity:</strong> handles large bowls and batch cooking</li><li><strong>Grams and ounces:</strong> easy unit switching</li><li><strong>Flat, wipe-clean surface:</strong> hygienic and simple</li></ul></div>

Everything beyond this is a bonus. Some scales add nutrition displays or app connectivity, which can be handy, but they are conveniences rather than essentials. A simple, accurate scale is all most people ever need.

## Grams vs ounces

Weigh in **grams** where you can. Most nutrition databases and recipes (including ours) list values per 100g, so grams make the maths simpler and the tracking more precise. A good scale switches units at the press of a button, but grams will save you time.

## How a scale fits your routine

- **Calorie tracking:** Weigh protein, carbs and fats to log accurate portions instead of guessing.
- **Meal prep:** Portion containers evenly so every meal hits the same macros.
- **Baking and cooking:** Weighing ingredients is far more reliable than cups, especially for baking.
- **Following recipes:** Our recipe nutrition is based on weighed amounts, so a scale lets you match them exactly. Browse the [recipes](/recipes/) to try it.
