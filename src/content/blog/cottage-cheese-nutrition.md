Cottage cheese has gone from diet-plate afterthought to viral superstar, and the reason is simple: it is one of the most protein-packed, versatile foods you can buy. Here is a clear breakdown of its nutrition, benefits, and exactly how much protein and calcium a serving delivers.

<div class="key-takeaway"><strong>The short answer</strong>Low-fat cottage cheese is very high in protein (~12g per 100g), low in calories (~72 per 100g) and a useful source of calcium (~83mg per 100g) and B12. A half-cup serving delivers around 14g protein, making it excellent for muscle, fullness and weight management.</div>

## Cottage cheese nutrition at a glance

Here is what low-fat cottage cheese provides, per 100 g and per typical 150 g serving:

| Nutrient | Per 100g | Per 150g serving |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Calories | ~72 | ~108 |
| Protein | ~12g | ~18g |
| Calcium | ~83mg | ~125mg |
| Carbs | ~3g | ~4.5g |
| Fat | ~1g | ~1.5g |

## Protein in cottage cheese

Protein is the headline. Cottage cheese delivers around **12 g per 100 g**, and a generous serving can provide **14 to 18 g**. It also contains **casein**, a slow-digesting protein that releases amino acids gradually, which is why many people eat it before bed.

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>Why the protein matters:</strong> it keeps you full, preserves muscle, and helps you hit your daily protein target affordably. A tub of cottage cheese is one of the cheapest ways to add serious protein to a meal.</p></div>

## Calcium and other nutrients

Cottage cheese provides around **83 mg of calcium per 100 g**, a useful contribution to bone health, plus **vitamin B12**, phosphorus and selenium. Its calcium is a little lower than Greek yogurt, but it still adds up across a serving. See our [high-calcium foods list](/food-lists/high-calcium-foods/) for the richest sources.

## Is cottage cheese good for you?

For most people, cottage cheese is an excellent choice:

- **Very high in protein** for muscle and fullness
- **Low in calories and fat**
- **Good source of calcium and B12**
- **Endlessly versatile** in sweet and savoury dishes

The one thing to watch is **sodium**, which can be high in some brands. If you are limiting salt, check the label and look for lower-sodium options.

## Why is cottage cheese so popular?

Cottage cheese has gone viral because it ticks every box of modern eating: **high protein, low cost, and incredible versatility**. People blend it smooth for creamy pasta sauces and dips, mix it into pancakes and flatbreads, and even turn it into high-protein ice cream. It delivers a protein hit in both sweet and savoury dishes, which fits perfectly with the focus on protein and affordable, healthy food. For ways to use it, see our [high-protein cottage cheese recipes and ideas](/blog/cottage-cheese-recipes/).

## Cottage cheese vs Greek yogurt

The two high-protein dairy stars are often compared. Cottage cheese is slightly higher in protein, while Greek yogurt is higher in calcium and creamier. For a full side-by-side, see our [cottage cheese vs Greek yogurt comparison](/compare/cottage-cheese-vs-greek-yogurt/).

## The bottom line

Cottage cheese is a nutritional powerhouse: very high in protein, low in calories and fat, and a good source of calcium and B12. A single serving delivers around 14 to 18 g of protein, supporting muscle, fullness and weight management, all at a low cost. Choose plain versions, watch the sodium, and enjoy it in everything from breakfast bowls to savoury dips.
