Fibre has quietly become the nutrient everyone is talking about. Dubbed "fibermaxxing" online, the idea is simple: most of us do not eat enough fibre, and deliberately eating more supports gut health, fullness and overall wellbeing. Here is what fibermaxxing actually means, how much you need, and how to do it without the bloating.

<div class="key-takeaway"><strong>The short answer</strong>Fibermaxxing means eating more whole-food fibre to hit around 30 to 35g a day, up from the ~15g many people manage. Focus on beans, lentils, vegetables, fruit, wholegrains, nuts and seeds. Increase gradually and drink plenty of water to avoid bloating.</div>

## Why fibre is having its moment

For years protein got all the attention. Now fibre is catching up, driven by two things: growing awareness of **gut health**, and the rise of **GLP-1 medications**, where fibre helps with fullness and digestion. Some now call fibre "the new protein", though the truth is you want both.

The reason the trend has substance: most people genuinely fall short. General guidance suggests **25 to 35g a day**, yet average intakes are often around 15g. That gap is what fibermaxxing sets out to close.

## How much fibre do you actually need?

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>Aim for around 30 to 35g a day</strong> for most adults. There is no need to chase very high numbers like 50g or more, which can cause digestive discomfort. Consistency at a sensible level beats occasional extremes.</p></div>

## The best foods for fibermaxxing

The key is **whole foods**, not powders. Here is where the most fibre comes from:

| Food group | Examples |
| --- | --- |
| Legumes | Beans, lentils, chickpeas |
| Wholegrains | Oats, wholewheat, rye |
| Seeds | Chia, flax |
| Vegetables | Broccoli, carrots, leafy greens |
| Fruit | Berries, apples, pears (with skin) |
| Nuts | Almonds, walnuts |

Our [high-fibre foods list](/food-lists/high-fiber-foods/) ranks the best sources by fibre content, and our [gut health foods](/blog/gut-health-foods/) guide shows how fibre and a healthy microbiome connect.

## How to fibremaxx without the bloating

The most common mistake is doing too much, too fast. Follow these principles:

- **Go gradually.** Increase fibre over a couple of weeks, not overnight.
- **Drink water.** Fibre needs fluid to do its job comfortably.
- **Spread it out.** Fibre at each meal is gentler than a single big hit.
- **Prefer whole foods.** Beans and vegetables are kinder than sudden doses of fibre powder.

## Don't forget protein

Fibermaxxing works best **alongside** enough protein, not instead of it. Protein preserves muscle and fibre supports fullness and digestion, a powerful pair for weight management and gut health. Set your protein target with our [protein calculator](/calculators/protein/), then build meals rich in both.

## The bottom line

Fibermaxxing is a rare trend with real substance: most people genuinely under-eat fibre, and closing that gap supports fullness, digestion and gut health. Aim for around 30 to 35g a day from whole foods, build up gradually with plenty of water, and pair it with protein.
