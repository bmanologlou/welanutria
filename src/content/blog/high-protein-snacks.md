Snacking has a bad reputation, but the problem is rarely snacking itself. It is what we reach for. Swap the biscuits and crisps for something with real protein and a snack stops being a setback and becomes one of the easiest ways to stay full, control cravings and hit your daily protein target.

<div class="key-takeaway"><strong>The short answer</strong>The best high-protein snacks give you at least 10 to 15 g of protein with little added sugar. Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, eggs, edamame and jerky are among the simplest and most filling options.</div>

## Why protein makes the best snack

Protein is the most filling of the three macronutrients. Gram for gram it satisfies hunger more than carbs or fat, which is exactly what you want between meals. A protein-rich snack steadies your appetite so you arrive at your next meal hungry but in control, rather than ravenous.

It also helps you hit your daily target. Most people load their protein into dinner and come up short earlier in the day. A couple of well-chosen snacks can quietly add 25 to 40 g, often the gap between missing and meeting your goal. Our [protein calculator](/calculators/protein/) shows you exactly what that target should be.

## Grab-and-go snacks (no cooking)

These need no preparation beyond opening a packet or a pot. Perfect for work, travel or a busy day.

| Snack | Protein (approx) |
| --- | --- |
| Greek yogurt (170 g pot) | 17 g |
| Cottage cheese (150 g) | 16 g |
| Two hard-boiled eggs | 13 g |
| Beef or turkey jerky (30 g) | 10 to 15 g |
| Edamame (100 g) | 11 g |
| String cheese (2 sticks) | 14 g |
| Tinned tuna (small tin) | 16 g |
| Ready-to-drink protein shake | 20 to 30 g |

## Simple homemade snacks

A little assembly, no real cooking, and far cheaper than packaged snacks.

- **Greek yogurt with berries and seeds.** Around 17 g protein, plus fibre and antioxidants. Our [cottage cheese and fruit bowl](/recipes/cottage-cheese-fruit-bowl/) follows the same idea.
- **Apple slices with peanut butter.** Protein and fibre together for lasting fullness.
- **Roasted chickpeas.** Crunchy, around 7 g protein per portion, a great crisp replacement.
- **Hummus with vegetable sticks.** Plant protein with fibre and a satisfying crunch.
- **Peanut butter energy balls.** Make a batch ahead. Try our [peanut butter energy balls](/recipes/peanut-butter-energy-balls/).
- **Cottage cheese on wholegrain toast.** Simple, savoury and around 18 g protein.

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>Pair protein with fibre.</strong> Protein keeps you full, and fibre extends that fullness further. Combinations like yogurt and berries, or apple and peanut butter, beat either one alone.</p></div>

## High-protein snacks for weight loss

If you are in a calorie deficit, the goal is maximum protein for minimum calories. These give you the most fullness per calorie:

- Greek yogurt (plain, not flavoured)
- Cottage cheese
- Boiled eggs or egg whites
- Edamame
- Turkey or chicken slices
- A protein shake made with water

These lean, high-protein options pair perfectly with a structured plan like our [weight loss meal plan](/meal-plans/weight-loss-meal-plan/). For more ideas, browse our [high-protein foods list](/food-lists/high-protein-foods/).

## Vegan high-protein snacks

Plant-based snacking is easy once you know where the protein is:

- Edamame (11 g per 100 g)
- Roasted chickpeas (7 g per portion)
- A handful of almonds or peanuts (6 g)
- Hummus with vegetables
- Soy yogurt
- Plant-based protein shake

See our [plant-based protein foods list](/food-lists/plant-based-protein-foods/) for more.

## How to choose a packaged snack

When you do reach for a bar or packaged snack, read the label. Look for at least **10 g of protein** and **under 10 g of added sugar**. Many "protein" bars are closer to confectionery, so the numbers matter more than the marketing on the front.

## The bottom line

High-protein snacks are one of the simplest upgrades you can make. They keep you full, curb cravings and help you hit a protein target you would otherwise miss. Keep a few easy options on hand, whether that is yogurt in the fridge or jerky in your bag, and snacking becomes part of the plan rather than a problem.
