"Calculating your macros" sounds technical, but it is really just three simple steps: find your calories, choose a split, and turn it into grams. Here is exactly how to do it, with a worked example, so you can set targets that actually match your goal.

## Step 1: Find your calories

Everything starts with your daily calorie target. This is your **TDEE** (total daily energy expenditure), adjusted for your goal:

- **Maintain:** eat at your TDEE
- **Lose fat:** subtract around 20% (or 500 calories)
- **Build muscle:** add around 10 to 15%

Our [TDEE calculator](/calculators/tdee/) works this out from your age, sex, height, weight and activity level.

## Step 2: Choose your macro split

A "split" is how you divide those calories between protein, carbs and fat. The most common ones:

| Split | Protein | Carbs | Fat | Best for |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Balanced | 30% | 40% | 30% | Most people |
| High-protein | 40% | 40% | 20% | Muscle, weight loss |
| Low-carb | 40% | 20% | 40% | Carb-sensitive |

Learn more about the popular high-protein option in our [40-40-20 diet guide](/blog/40-40-20-diet/).

## Step 3: Convert to grams

This is the only maths, and it is simple. Each macro has a calorie value per gram:

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>Protein:</strong> 4 calories per gram<br><strong>Carbs:</strong> 4 calories per gram<br><strong>Fat:</strong> 9 calories per gram</p></div>

To get grams: multiply your calories by each percentage, then divide by the calories per gram.

## A worked example

Let's say your target is **2,000 calories** on a **balanced** (30/40/30) split:

- **Protein:** 2,000 × 30% = 600 cal ÷ 4 = **150g**
- **Carbs:** 2,000 × 40% = 800 cal ÷ 4 = **200g**
- **Fat:** 2,000 × 30% = 600 cal ÷ 9 = **67g**

That's it. Three numbers to aim for each day.
