Most protein advice is written as if everyone is a young male gym-goer. But protein matters just as much for women, and in some life stages even more. From preserving muscle to easing the changes of menopause, getting enough protein is one of the most useful things a woman can do for her long-term health.

<div class="key-takeaway"><strong>The short answer</strong>Most women benefit from more protein than the bare minimum. Active women, those losing fat, and women over 50 do well on around 1.4 to 2.0 g per kilo of body weight, roughly 90 to 130 g a day for a 65 kg woman.</div>

## How much protein women actually need

The baseline recommendation of 0.8 g per kilo of body weight is the amount needed to prevent deficiency, not the amount for thriving. Most women do better above it.

| Situation | Protein per kilo | For a 65 kg woman |
| --- | --- | --- |
| General health (sedentary) | 0.8 g | ~52 g |
| Active / regular exercise | 1.4 to 1.6 g | ~90 to 105 g |
| Losing fat (keep muscle) | 1.6 to 2.0 g | ~105 to 130 g |
| Over 50 / menopause | 1.2 to 2.0 g | ~80 to 130 g |

Our [protein calculator](/calculators/protein/) gives you a personalised target based on your weight, activity and goal.

## Do women need less than men?

In total grams, often yes, because women tend to carry less muscle and weigh less. But **per kilo of body weight the requirement is essentially the same**. What really drives your protein needs is your muscle mass, how active you are and what you are trying to achieve, not your sex on its own.

## Protein and weight loss

Protein is the most useful nutrient when you are trying to lose weight, for two reasons. It is the most filling macronutrient, so it controls hunger in a calorie deficit, and it preserves muscle while you lose fat, which keeps your metabolism higher. The result is that more of the weight you lose is fat rather than muscle.

This is why most successful weight-loss approaches lean on protein. Our [calorie deficit calculator](/calculators/calorie-deficit/) shows your target, and a higher protein intake makes hitting it far more comfortable.

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>Protein protects your results.</strong> Losing weight without enough protein means losing muscle alongside fat, which lowers your metabolism and makes the weight easier to regain. Protein is what protects the muscle you want to keep.</p></div>

## Protein around menopause

Menopause brings hormonal changes that speed up muscle and bone loss and can shift body fat toward the midsection. Protein helps on every front. It preserves muscle, supports bone health alongside calcium and vitamin D, and keeps appetite steady at a time when it can otherwise rise.

Research suggests even modest increases in protein can help offset these changes. For many women, simply nudging protein up at breakfast and adding a protein-rich snack is enough to make a real difference. Protein is just one piece, though: see our full guide to the [best foods for menopause](/blog/best-foods-for-menopause/) for calcium, fibre and more.

## Best protein foods for women

A varied mix covers protein and the other nutrients women often need more of, like iron and calcium:

- **Eggs**, a complete protein with iron and vitamin D
- **Greek yogurt and cottage cheese**, high protein plus calcium
- **Chicken, fish and lean meat**, protein-dense and rich in iron
- **Tofu, edamame and beans**, plant protein with fibre
- **Lentils**, protein, fibre and iron together

## The bottom line

Protein is not just for athletes or men. For women, it supports weight management, protects muscle and bone, and eases the transition through menopause. Most women benefit from eating more than the minimum, spread across the day. Work out your target, lead each meal with a protein source, and the rest follows.
