Protein gets the headlines and fibre is having its moment, but the real magic happens when a food delivers **both at once**. High-protein, high-fibre foods are the workhorses of a healthy diet: they keep you fuller than either nutrient alone, support muscle and gut health, and make weight management far easier. Here are the best of them, and how to build meals around them.

<div class="key-takeaway"><strong>The short answer</strong>The best high-protein high-fibre foods are legumes (beans, lentils, chickpeas, edamame), seeds (chia, flax, pumpkin, hemp), soy foods (tofu, natto) and wholegrains (oats, rye). Together, protein and fibre keep you fuller on fewer calories, supporting weight, muscle and gut health at once.</div>

## Why protein and fibre are better together

These two nutrients are the most **satiating** things you can eat, and they work through different mechanisms:

- **Protein** preserves muscle, curbs appetite and has the highest "thermic effect" (your body burns more digesting it).
- **Fibre** slows digestion, steadies blood sugar, and feeds the beneficial bacteria in your gut.

Put them in the same meal and you get powerful, long-lasting fullness, the reason this combination is central to weight management, [fibermaxxing](/blog/fibermaxxing/) and [GLP-1 friendly eating](/blog/glp-1-friendly-foods/) alike.

## The best high-protein high-fibre foods

The clear winners are plant foods, especially legumes, since animal proteins contain no fibre at all.

| Food | Protein | Fibre |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lentils (cooked) | ~9g | ~8g |
| Chickpeas (cooked) | ~9g | ~8g |
| Black beans (cooked) | ~9g | ~9g |
| Edamame | ~12g | ~5g |
| Chia seeds | ~17g | ~34g |
| Pumpkin seeds | ~30g | ~6g |
| Oats (rolled) | ~17g | ~11g |
| Natto | ~19g | ~5g |

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>Legumes are the everyday champions.</strong> Beans, lentils and chickpeas give you protein and fibre in near-equal measure, are cheap, versatile and filling. If you do one thing, eat more of them.</p></div>

## Easy ways to combine them

You do not need recipes, just combinations. Some simple high-protein, high-fibre pairings:

- **Overnight oats + chia seeds + Greek yogurt** (a triple hit)
- **Lentil or bean soup** with a wholegrain roll
- **Chickpea salad** with seeds and leafy greens
- **Tofu stir-fry** with edamame and brown rice
- **Greek yogurt + berries + a spoon of pumpkin seeds**

Our [chickpeas vs lentils comparison](/compare/chickpeas-vs-lentils/) can help you pick between the two everyday staples, and our [recipes](/recipes/) put many of these combinations to work.

## How much should you aim for?

A useful target is around **1.2 to 1.6g of protein per kilo** of body weight and roughly **30g of fibre** a day. Most people fall short on both, so foods that provide them together make hitting your numbers far simpler. Work out your personal protein target with our [protein calculator](/calculators/protein/).

## The bottom line

If you want one simple upgrade to your diet, eat more foods that are high in **both** protein and fibre. Legumes, seeds, soy foods and wholegrains keep you fuller for longer, support muscle and gut health, and make weight management easier, all at once. Start with our [high-protein high-fibre foods list](/food-lists/high-protein-high-fiber-foods/) and build from there.
