GLP-1 medications like semaglutide have changed how millions of people eat, by sharply reducing appetite. When you are eating less, **what** you eat matters more than ever: every bite needs to deliver protein, fibre and nutrients. This guide covers the food principles that help, whatever the reason you are eating smaller portions.

<div class="note"><strong>Important:</strong> This is general nutrition information, not medical advice. If you are taking a GLP-1 medication, follow the guidance of your prescribing doctor or a registered dietitian for your individual needs.</div>

<div class="key-takeaway"><strong>The short answer</strong>When appetite is reduced, prioritise protein (to preserve muscle) and fibre (for fullness and digestion), and make every bite nutrient-dense. Lean meats, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, skyr, beans, lentils, tofu, vegetables and berries are ideal. Limit greasy, fried and very sugary foods, which are often harder to tolerate.</div>

## Why protein comes first

The single most important principle: **protein preserves muscle**. When you lose weight quickly on reduced appetite, some of that loss can come from muscle unless you get enough protein. Prioritising it at every meal protects lean tissue, keeps you stronger, and supports metabolism.

Because portions are smaller, choose **protein-dense** foods so a little goes a long way:

| Food | Protein (per 100g) |
| --- | --- |
| Chicken breast | ~31g |
| Skyr / Greek yogurt | ~11g / ~10g |
| Eggs | ~13g |
| Lentils (cooked) | ~9g |
| Tofu | ~8g |

Not sure how much you need? Our [protein calculator](/calculators/protein/) gives a personalised target, and our [high-protein foods list](/food-lists/high-protein-foods/) ranks the best sources.

## Why fibre comes a close second

Fibre does two key jobs here: it **supports fullness** (helping small meals satisfy) and **aids digestion** (important, since constipation is a common GLP-1 side effect). This is where the "fibermaxxing" idea and GLP-1 eating overlap.

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>Increase fibre gradually</strong> and drink plenty of water. Going from low to very high fibre overnight can cause bloating. Build up slowly with vegetables, beans, lentils, wholegrains, nuts and seeds.</p></div>

See our [high-fibre foods list](/food-lists/high-fiber-foods/) for the best sources, and our [gut health foods](/blog/gut-health-foods/) guide, since fibre and gut health go hand in hand.

## Make every bite count

With a smaller appetite, "empty" calories are a wasted opportunity. Aim for **nutrient-dense** foods that bring vitamins and minerals alongside protein and fibre:

- **Proteins:** eggs, fish, chicken, turkey, Greek yogurt, skyr, tofu, beans
- **Fibre and colour:** vegetables, berries, wholegrains, lentils
- **Healthy fats (in moderation):** olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds

## What many people find easier to limit

These are not strict rules, but foods that GLP-1 users often report tolerating less well:

- **Greasy and fried foods** (can trigger nausea)
- **Very large portions** (uncomfortable on a reduced appetite)
- **Sugary drinks and ultra-processed snacks** (low in the protein and fibre that matter)

The goal is not restriction for its own sake, but making your limited appetite work for you.

## The bottom line

Eating well on a GLP-1 medication comes down to a simple shift: since you are eating less, make it count. **Protein first, fibre close behind, nutrient-dense whole foods throughout.** These same principles help anyone eating smaller portions.
