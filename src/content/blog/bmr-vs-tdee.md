If you have used a calorie calculator, you have probably seen both **BMR** and **TDEE**. They are related but not the same, and knowing the difference helps you set the right calorie target. Here is a clear, simple explanation.

<div class="key-takeaway"><strong>The short answer</strong>BMR is the calories you burn at complete rest. TDEE is your BMR plus the calories you burn through activity and exercise. BMR is the baseline; TDEE is your full daily burn, and it's the number you use to manage your weight.</div>

## What is BMR?

Your **Basal Metabolic Rate** is the energy your body uses at complete rest, just to keep you alive: breathing, circulation, cell repair, brain function. It is the largest single chunk of your daily calorie burn, usually **60 to 70 percent**.

BMR is calculated from your age, sex, height and weight, most accurately with the **Mifflin-St Jeor equation**. Work yours out with our [BMR calculator](/calculators/bmr/).

## What is TDEE?

Your **Total Daily Energy Expenditure** is everything you burn in a day, your BMR **plus** all your movement: walking, fidgeting, workouts, even digesting food. It is calculated by multiplying your BMR by an **activity factor**:

| Activity level | Factor |
| --- | --- |
| Sedentary | 1.2 |
| Lightly active | 1.375 |
| Moderately active | 1.55 |
| Very active | 1.725 |
| Extremely active | 1.9 |

Our [TDEE calculator](/calculators/tdee/) does this for you and also gives you a macro split.

## How they relate

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>TDEE = BMR × activity factor.</strong> BMR is the resting foundation; TDEE builds on it by adding everything you do. That's why TDEE is always higher than BMR, from about 20% higher if you're sedentary to nearly double if you're very active.</p></div>

## Which should you use?

For everyday nutrition, **use TDEE**. It reflects the calories you actually burn, so:

- **Maintain weight:** eat around your TDEE
- **Lose fat:** eat below your TDEE (a deficit)
- **Build muscle:** eat slightly above your TDEE

Eating below your **BMR** is generally too aggressive, hard to sustain and can cost muscle. Create your deficit from TDEE instead. Our [calorie deficit calculator](/calculators/calorie-deficit/) can help.

## The bottom line

BMR is your resting calorie burn; TDEE is your total daily burn once activity is included. You calculate BMR first, then multiply by activity to get TDEE, and TDEE is the number you use to manage your weight.
