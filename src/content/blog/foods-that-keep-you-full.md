Hunger is the reason most diets fail. Cut calories without thinking about fullness and you spend the day fighting your appetite, which rarely ends well. The smarter approach is to choose foods that fill you up on fewer calories, so eating less stops feeling like a battle.

<div class="key-takeaway"><strong>The short answer</strong>The most filling foods are high in protein, fibre or water and low in calorie density. Eggs, Greek yogurt, oats, beans, potatoes, soups and leafy greens let you eat a satisfying amount while staying in a calorie deficit.</div>

## Why some foods fill you up more

Three things make a food filling, and the best foods combine more than one.

- **Protein.** The most satiating macronutrient. It slows digestion and triggers the hormones that tell your brain you are full.
- **Fibre.** Adds bulk, slows digestion and feeds your gut. High-fibre foods take longer to eat and longer to leave your stomach.
- **Water and volume.** Foods with a high water content, like soups, fruit and vegetables, fill your stomach for very few calories.

The key idea underneath all of this is **calorie density**, the number of calories in a given weight of food. Low-calorie-dense foods let you eat a big, satisfying plate for relatively few calories. High-calorie-dense foods, like oils and sweets, pack a lot of calories into a small bite.

## The 15 most filling foods

| Food | Why it fills you up |
| --- | --- |
| Eggs | High protein, very satiating |
| Greek yogurt | High protein, creamy volume |
| Boiled potatoes | Top-ranked for fullness per calorie |
| Oats | Soluble fibre that thickens and slows digestion |
| Beans | Protein and fibre together |
| Lentils | High fibre, high plant protein |
| Chicken breast | Lean, protein-dense |
| Fish | High protein, low calorie |
| Cottage cheese | High protein, low calorie |
| Apples | Fibre and water, low calorie |
| Berries | High volume, high fibre |
| Leafy greens | Huge volume for almost no calories |
| Broth-based soups | Water and fibre fill the stomach |
| Popcorn (air-popped) | High volume, whole grain |
| Chia seeds | Absorb water and expand, very filling |

## Build meals around them

You do not need to eat these foods on their own. The trick is to build meals where protein and fibre lead and calorie-dense extras play a supporting role.

## How this helps weight loss

Filling foods do not melt fat directly. What they do is make a calorie deficit sustainable. When you are satisfied, you eat less without willpower running the show, and it is that deficit, kept up over time, that drives weight loss. Our [calorie deficit calculator](/calculators/calorie-deficit/) shows you the target, and these foods make hitting it far easier.

## The bottom line

If you want to eat less without feeling hungry, change what is on your plate before you cut the portion size. Lead with protein, fibre and high-volume foods, keep the calorie-dense extras modest, and fullness does most of the work for you.
