Zinc does not get the same attention as vitamin C or magnesium, but it quietly supports some of the body's most important functions, from immunity to healing to healthy skin. Because your body cannot store it, getting enough regularly matters. Here is what zinc does, how much you need, and the best foods to find it in.

## What zinc does

Zinc is involved in a wide range of essential processes. The best known is supporting the **immune system**, but it also drives **wound healing**, **skin health**, **growth**, **DNA production** and your **sense of taste and smell**. It even plays a role in hormone health.

Because the body has no real store of zinc, you need a regular supply from food. A shortfall tends to show up gradually, in things like more frequent infections or slower healing.

## How much do you need?

| Group | Zinc per day |
| --- | --- |
| Adult men | ~11 mg |
| Adult women | ~8 mg |
| Pregnancy | ~11 to 12 mg |

Most people who eat a varied diet reach this comfortably. Our [protein calculator](/calculators/protein/) is useful here too, since many high-protein foods, like meat, shellfish and legumes, are also among the best zinc sources.

## Best zinc-rich foods

One food stands far above the rest, then a range of meat, seafood and plant options follow.

- **Oysters**, by far the richest source of all
- **Red meat** like beef and lamb
- **Crab** and other **shellfish**
- **Pumpkin seeds** and **hemp seeds**
- **Pine nuts** and other nuts
- **Lentils**, **chickpeas** and other legumes
- **Wholegrains**

For the full ranking, see our [high-zinc foods list](/food-lists/high-zinc-foods/).

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>Oysters are in a league of their own.</strong> A single serving of oysters can provide several times your daily zinc. If you do not eat them, red meat, seeds and legumes together cover your needs well.</p></div>

## Zinc for vegetarians

Plant foods do contain zinc, but it is absorbed a little less efficiently than zinc from animal foods. The practical fix is simple: include zinc-rich plants regularly, and use preparation methods that improve absorption.

- Lean on **pumpkin and hemp seeds**, **nuts**, **legumes**, **tofu**, **tempeh** and **wholegrains**
- **Soaking or sprouting** legumes and grains improves how well the zinc is absorbed
- Our [plant-based protein foods list](/food-lists/plant-based-protein-foods/) overlaps well, since many plant proteins are also good zinc sources

## Zinc, immunity and supplements

Zinc's link to immunity is well established, and some research suggests it may slightly shorten colds, though the evidence is mixed. Meeting your needs through food is the sensible foundation. High-dose supplements can cause nausea and, over time, interfere with copper absorption, so they should be used carefully and ideally on professional advice.

## The bottom line

Zinc is a quiet but essential mineral for immunity, healing and skin, and because the body cannot store it, regular intake matters. Most people get enough from a varied diet, whether through oysters and red meat or seeds, nuts and legumes.
