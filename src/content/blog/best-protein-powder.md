## Do you actually need protein powder?

Start here, because it is the honest question. Protein powder is a **convenience, not a necessity**. If you can reach your protein target through whole foods, you do not need it. What powder does well is make topping up **easy and affordable**, especially after workouts or on busy days when cooking is not an option.

Not sure what your target is? Use our [protein calculator](/calculators/protein/) to get a personalised number based on your body weight and goals. Then aim to hit most of it from food, and use powder to close any gap.

## The main types compared

| Type | Best for | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Whey concentrate | All-round value | Fast-absorbing, popular, budget-friendly |
| Whey isolate | Lower carbs/lactose | More filtered, higher protein %, pricier |
| Casein | Night-time, long gaps | Slow-digesting, keeps you fuller |
| Plant (pea, soy, rice) | Vegans, dairy-free | Blends give a complete amino profile |

**Whey** is the default choice for most people: it is well-studied, absorbed quickly, and offers the best value. **Casein** suits those who want a slow-release option before bed. **Plant proteins** have come a long way, and a good blend (commonly pea plus rice) provides a complete amino acid profile that rivals whey for everyday use.

## What to look for on the label

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>The 4 things that matter most:</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Protein per scoop:</strong> aim for 20 to 25g</li><li><strong>Added sugar:</strong> ideally under 3g</li><li><strong>Short ingredient list:</strong> mostly protein, minimal fillers</li><li><strong>Clear labelling:</strong> avoid hidden "proprietary blends"</li></ul></div>

Everything else (flavours, added vitamins, fancy marketing) is secondary. A good protein powder is mostly protein, with low sugar and nothing to hide.

## How to choose for your goal

- **Building muscle:** A whey concentrate or isolate around 24g protein per scoop, taken around training or as a snack. Pair with our [TDEE calculator](/calculators/tdee/) to make sure you are eating enough overall.
- **Weight loss:** A lower-calorie isolate or a lean plant protein helps you stay full and hit protein while keeping calories down. See our [foods that keep you full](/blog/foods-that-keep-you-full/) guide too.
- **Vegan or dairy-free:** A pea-and-rice blend gives a complete amino profile without dairy.
- **General health:** Any quality powder that fits your taste and budget. You do not need the most expensive option.

## The bottom line

The "best" protein powder is the one that fits your goal, tastes good enough that you will actually use it, and has a clean label. For most people that is a solid whey; for those avoiding dairy, a good plant blend. Whatever you choose, remember that powder is there to **top up** a diet built on whole foods, not replace it.
