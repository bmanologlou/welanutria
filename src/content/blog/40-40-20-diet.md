The "40-40-20 diet" keeps popping up in macro-tracking and fitness circles, especially for breakfast ideas. It is not really a diet in the restrictive sense, but a **macronutrient split**. Here is exactly what it means, how to work out your numbers, and easy meals that fit.

<div class="key-takeaway"><strong>The short answer</strong>40-40-20 means splitting your daily calories as 40% carbs, 40% protein and 20% fat. The high protein share supports muscle and fullness, making it popular for weight loss and body recomposition. Total calories still drive results; the split just helps you stay full and keep muscle.</div>

## What does 40-40-20 mean?

It refers to the share of your daily **calories** from each macronutrient:

- **40% carbohydrates** (energy and training fuel)
- **40% protein** (muscle and fullness)
- **20% fat** (hormones and essentials)

The standout feature is the **high protein share**. Most general diets sit around 15 to 25 percent protein, so 40 percent is deliberately protein-forward, which is why it appeals to people focused on muscle and fat loss.

## How to calculate your 40-40-20 macros

<div class="highlight"><p><strong>Step 1:</strong> Find your daily calorie target (use our <a href="/calculators/tdee/">TDEE calculator</a>).<br><strong>Step 2:</strong> Split the calories 40/40/20.<br><strong>Step 3:</strong> Convert to grams: carbs and protein have 4 calories per gram, fat has 9.</p></div>

For example, on a **2,000 calorie** day:

| Macro | % | Calories | Grams |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Carbs | 40% | 800 | ~200g |
| Protein | 40% | 800 | ~200g |
| Fat | 20% | 400 | ~44g |

Our [TDEE and macro calculator](/calculators/tdee/) does the calorie maths for you, then you apply the split.

## Is it good for weight loss?

It can be, for one main reason: the **high protein** share helps you stay full and preserve muscle while eating in a calorie deficit. That said, no split creates weight loss on its own, total calories still decide that. The 40-40-20 ratio simply makes a deficit easier to sustain and more muscle-sparing.

## Easy 40-40-20 meal ideas

The tricky part is usually hitting 40% protein, so lean protein anchors every meal:

- **Breakfast:** Greek yogurt or [skyr](/blog/skyr-nutrition/) with berries and oats; or eggs with wholegrain toast
- **Lunch:** Chicken or tofu with rice and vegetables
- **Dinner:** Fish or lean meat with potatoes and salad
- **Snacks:** Cottage cheese, a protein smoothie, or edamame

[High-protein high-fibre foods](/food-lists/high-protein-high-fiber-foods/) are especially useful here, since they help you hit the protein target while keeping you full. Browse our [high-protein foods list](/food-lists/high-protein-foods/) and [recipes](/recipes/) for more.

## The bottom line

The 40-40-20 diet is simply a protein-forward macro split: 40% carbs, 40% protein, 20% fat. It suits active people and anyone who finds high protein keeps them fullest, and it can support weight loss by protecting muscle in a deficit. Start with your calorie target from our [TDEE calculator](/calculators/tdee/), then build protein-rich meals around the ratio.
